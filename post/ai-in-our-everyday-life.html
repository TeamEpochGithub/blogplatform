AI in our Everyday Life: How & Where?

Kerem Bayraktar

Mar 16, 2022

In its very early days, Artificial Intelligence was thought of as a way of replacing the human thinking process. For example, considered as the first AI program the “” was used to prove known mathematical proofs stated in Bertrand Russell’s famous work “Principia Mathematica”. Another example of AI being used to replace human logic is a  against human opponents. It was developed by Arthur Samuel in 1959 on the IBM 704 computer. However, as researchers continued to investigate the possible use of AI in our society, a lot of obstacles arose. In addition to the lack of enough computational power at its time, the best result was not always calculated within a reasonable timeframe. Although there were ups and downs within the field in the following years, it became apparent that the idea of a “supercomputer in charge of our society” was still in the distant future. Through time, the perception of AI changed in a way that it doesn’t necessarily challenge the usefulness of human intelligence but rather be used as an accelerator tool for decision making. With the shift to the digital age, the amount of data that we create has increased drastically. This created a demand for how this unused flow of data could be salvaged through analysis to improve efficiency in certain fields. Therefore, AI became the transition step since it doesn’t require as many resources to allocate while still keeping a level of intelligence that is usually lost through automation.

Currently, AI has a wide range of use in today’s society. Most importantly, combined with specialists in their respective fields that are aware of the limitations, AI can provide applicable solutions to various exhaustive issues that we come across in our everyday lives. AI is widely used in finance technologies. Artificial Intelligence will help to make predictions about the future with regard to the old data that is presented. This plays a crucial role in the stock exchange and especially in high-frequency trading, where machines are starting to replace human decision-making to minimize the risks. Not only limited to the stock exchange but Artificial Intelligence also has an important use in fraud detection. As they can see abnormal patterns that are not directly recognizable from outside, they become far more than their “human colleagues”. Besides the economic part of our lives,  AI can also be very helpful for protecting national security. For example, uses AI to develop sustainable solutions for foreign affairs and to have a better grasp of current debates in everyday politics(). In addition to risk evaluation and discovering feasible policies, it also has an important use in big data analysis that will help people to understand power dynamics in our societies better.

Although the days of AI replacing physicians is in the distant future (or is it ?), AI also plays an important role in assisting with the diagnosis of patients. Especially by recognizing patterns among patients with similar symptoms, as well as ease the process of diagnosis through the MRI and X-Ray results. This allows a certain level of predictability which saves time and allocates the right resources for diagnosis. One of our new competitions “” is a great example of this, as the competition aims to train algorithms for better classification of chemical compounds in the given blood sample. As there is a need for greater amounts of data to make more accurate predictions, eliminating the human factor and utilizing AI’s ability to systematically approach patterns in big data is crucial for optimizing this process.

AI is quite useful as a tool for pioneers of science too, and it’s also paving the path for further collaborations among the researchers of different disciplines. As it turned out to be very handy for everyday issues, researchers started to search different ways of its applicability to solve their very own research topics. Ranging from theoretical physics to nanobiology, it can provide the necessary intelligence and classification capability at a steady rate. Observing billions of stars through galaxies and analyze them. This is made with the help of AI by classifying celestial objects and training algorithms on their images taken by space telescopes. Apart from its availability for research purposes, AI is beneficial even for the entertainment sector such as sports. Modern football teams are frequently using systems built on artificial intelligence to track their players on the field and how they could improve further on their positioning and decision making for example. In addition to providing to teams problems, algorithms can help individual players to pinpoint their weak abilities and how to improve on those.
